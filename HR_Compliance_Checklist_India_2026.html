SECTION 01
Company & Employment Setup
Foundational registrations and licenses required before hiring any employee in India
Critical
Section Progress
0%
⚠️
Non-compliance Alert
Operating without mandatory registrations (EPF, ESIC, PAN/TAN) can attract penalties of ₹5,000–₹1,00,000+ per default, plus criminal liability for directors. MCA and GST authorities conduct random audits of startups.
Core Registrations
Certificate of Incorporation (MCA)
Filed with Ministry of Corporate Affairs. Required before any business activity. Obtain CIN (Corporate Identity Number). Keep certified copies accessible.
Companies Act 2013Penalty: ₹10,000+
ONE-TIME
PAN (Permanent Account Number) Registration
Mandatory entity PAN for all tax filings. Separate from promoters' personal PAN. Required for bank account opening and TDS compliance.
Income Tax Act 1961Penalty: ₹10,000
ONE-TIME
TAN (Tax Deduction Account Number) Registration
Required for all employers who deduct TDS on salaries. Must be quoted on all TDS challans, TDS certificates, and returns. Apply via Form 49B.
Income Tax Act §203APenalty: ₹10,000/default
ONE-TIME
GST Registration (if applicable)
Mandatory if annual turnover exceeds ₹20L (services) or ₹40L (goods). Required if providing services inter-state. Voluntary registration also available for smaller startups.
GST Act 2017Penalty: 10% of tax due
ONE-TIME
Professional Tax (PT) Registration
State-level obligation. Applicable in Maharashtra, Karnataka, West Bengal, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Gujarat, and others. Must register within 30 days of hiring first employee. Deduct and remit monthly/annually as applicable.
State PT ActsVaries by state
ONE-TIME + MONTHLY
Shops & Establishments Act Registration
State-specific. Must register within 30 days of commencement of business. Governs working hours, leave policies, and workplace conditions. Required for bank accounts in many states.
State S&E Acts₹2,000–₹20,000
ANNUAL RENEWAL
MSME / Udyam Registration
Optional but highly beneficial. Provides access to credit guarantee, priority lending, government tenders, and GST subsidies. Register on Udyam portal. No fee required.
MSME Act 2006
ONE-TIME
DPIIT Startup India Recognition
Unlock tax exemptions under Sec 80-IAC, Angel Tax relief, and labour law self-certification. Apply on Startup India portal. Valid for 10 years from incorporation.
DPIIT NotificationNEW BENEFIT 2026
ONE-TIME
Labour Identification Number (LIN)
Unique identifier for all labour-related filings on Shram Suvidha Portal. Required for EPF, ESIC, and other statutory filings. Register before first payroll cycle.
Shram Suvidha Portal

Professional Tax rates, Shops & Establishment rules, and applicable minimum wages differ significantly across states. Consult a local labour law consultant for Karnataka (Bengaluru), Maharashtra (Mumbai), Delhi-NCR, and Telangana jurisdictions.

Monthly & Ongoing Obligations
EPF Registration & Monthly Contribution
Mandatory when headcount crosses 20. Employee contributes 12% of basic+DA; employer contributes 12% (8.33% to EPS, 3.67% to EPF). File ECR monthly on EPFO portal. Generate UAN for all eligible employees.
EPF Act 1952Penalty: ₹5,000 + 12% interestDue: 15th
ESIC Registration & Monthly Contribution
Applies when headcount ≥10 (in most states) and employee salary ≤₹21,000/month. Employee: 0.75%; Employer: 3.25% of gross wages. File monthly on ESIC portal. Issue ESIC cards to employees.
ESI Act 1948Penalty: ₹5,000 + interestDue: 15th
TDS Deduction on Salaries (Section 192)
Compute TDS based on projected annual salary and applicable slab. Collect Form 12BB from employees for exemptions/deductions. Deposit by 7th of each month. Issue Form 16 by 15 June annually.
Income Tax §192Interest 1.5%/monthDue: 7th
Salary Slip Issuance
Issue monthly salary slips showing gross pay, all deductions (EPF, ESIC, PT, TDS), and net pay. Must be issued before or on payday. Maintain records for 8 years.
Payment of Wages Act
Form 12BB Collection (Employee Declaration)
Collect at start of FY. Includes HRA, LTA, Section 80C/80D investments, home loan interest. Update mid-year if employee's situation changes. Retain for TDS computation.
Income Tax RulesApril each year
Quarterly TDS Return (Form 24Q)
File quarterly TDS returns reflecting all salary TDS. Q1: 31 Jul; Q2: 31 Oct; Q3: 31 Jan; Q4: 31 May. Use TRACES portal. Reconcile challan with return before filing.
Income Tax §200₹200/day default
Form 16 Distribution
Annual TDS certificate issued to all employees by 15 June. Part A from TRACES (challan details); Part B (salary breakup and deductions) prepared by employer. Mandatory even if no TDS was deducted.
Income Tax §203₹100/day delay15 June
Professional Tax Deduction & Remittance
Deduct PT from employee salaries per state-specific slab (e.g., Maharashtra: ₹200/month for salaries ≥₹10,000). Remit to state authority. File annual PT return.
State PT Acts
New Tax Regime (Section 115BAC) — Default 2026
From FY 2024–25 onwards, new tax regime is the default. Employees must opt-in to old regime via Form 10-IEA. Payroll software must handle both regimes. Update TDS computation accordingly.
Finance Act 2023NEW DEFAULT

Labour Law Compliance
Key central and state labour legislation applicable to Indian startups
High Priority
Section Progress
0%
⚠️
Labour Codes — Transition Status
India's four Labour Codes (Wages, Industrial Relations, Social Security, OSH) have been passed but not yet fully notified for implementation as of 2026. The existing laws (listed below) remain in force. Monitor MoLE notifications closely for applicability timelines.
Shops & Establishments Act Compliance
Comply with state-specific rules on working hours (typically 9 hrs/day, 48 hrs/week), rest intervals, weekly holidays, overtime rates, and leave entitlements. Display registration certificate at premises.
State S&E Acts₹5,000–₹25,000
ONGOING
Payment of Wages Act Compliance
Pay wages within 7 days after wage period (small organisations) or by 10th of following month. No unauthorised deductions. Only authorised deductions (EPF, ESIC, PT, fines up to 3% of wages) are permitted.
Payment of Wages Act 1936₹3,750–₹22,500
MONTHLY
Minimum Wages Act Compliance
Pay at or above the state-notified minimum wage for each employee category/skill level. Wages revised twice a year (April & October) in most states. Non-payment is a criminal offence. Display rates at workplace.
Minimum Wages Act 1948Criminal: 6 months imprisonment
REVIEWED BIANNUALLY
Payment of Bonus Act Compliance
Applicable to establishments with 20+ employees. Eligible employees: salary ≤₹21,000/month; employed for ≥30 working days. Minimum bonus: 8.33% of annual salary; maximum: 20%. Pay before Diwali or within 8 months of year-end.
Bonus Act 1965₹1,000–₹6 months imprisonmentWithin 8 months of FY
Payment of Gratuity Act Compliance
Applicable to 10+ employee establishments. Gratuity = 15/26 × last drawn salary × years of service. Payable after 5 years of continuous service (exception: death/disability). Maximum: ₹20,00,000. Pay within 30 days of due date.
Gratuity Act 197210% p.a. interest on delayed payment
Industrial Disputes Act — Retrenchment & Closure Rules
For organisations with 100+ employees: prior government permission needed for layoffs and closure. Retrenchment compensation = 15 days wages for each year of service. 60 days written notice required.
ID Act 1947Criminal liability
Contract Labour Act (CLRA) Compliance
If engaging contract workers through contractors: register as principal employer if 20+ contract workers. Ensure contractors are licensed. Maintain register of contractors. Ensure contractors pay minimum wages and EPF/ESIC.
CLRA Act 1970₹1,000 + imprisonment
Four Labour Codes — Readiness Assessment
Conduct internal assessment for readiness under: Code on Wages, Industrial Relations Code, Code on Social Security, and OSH Code. Update payroll structures, leave policies, and standing orders accordingly.

SECTION 05
POSH Compliance
Prevention of Sexual Harassment at Workplace — mandatory for ALL employers
Critical
Section Progress
0%
🚨
Zero Exceptions
POSH Act applies to ALL organisations regardless of size — even those with a single employee. Non-compliance attracts ₹50,000 penalty and cancellation of business licence on second offence. Courts have taken a strict view of violations.
Internal Committee (IC) Formation
Mandatory for organisations with 10+ employees. IC must have: Presiding Officer (senior woman employee), minimum 2 members from employees, and 1 external member. At least 50% must be women. Terms of 3 years.
POSH Act 2013 §4₹50,000
External Member Appointment
The external member must be from an NGO/association committed to women's causes, OR a person familiar with sexual harassment issues. Document their qualifications and appointment letter.
POSH Act 2013 §4(2)(c)
POSH Policy Document
Draft and implement a comprehensive POSH Policy covering definition of sexual harassment, complaint mechanism, inquiry process, confidentiality obligations, penalties, and appeal process. Legal review recommended.
POSH Act 2013 §19
POSH Awareness Training — All Employees
Conduct mandatory training for all employees on definition of sexual harassment, complaint process, and IC contact. Conduct annually at minimum. Maintain attendance records. Online training acceptable post-COVID precedent.
POSH Act 2013 §19(c)Annual
IC Member POSH Training
IC members require specialised training on conducting inquiries, evidence handling, natural justice principles, and report writing. Document training completion for all IC members.
POSH Act 2013On appointment + Annual
Annual POSH Report Filing
IC must submit Annual Report to employer AND District Officer before 31 January each year. Report must include: number of complaints received, disposed, pending, and nature of action taken.
POSH Act 2013 §21Penalty for non-filing31 January
Annual Report Inclusion in Board Report
Companies Act requires the Board's Annual Report to include a statement on POSH compliance — number of cases filed and disposed under the POSH Act.
Companies Act 2013Annual
Workplace Notice / Poster Display
Display name and contact details of IC members, complaint mechanism, and penal consequences at prominent places in the workplace. Must be visible to all employees including contract workers.
POSH Act §19(b)
SECTION 06
Statutory Registers & Records
Mandatory registers to be maintained and available for inspection at all times
Standard
Section Progress
0%
📋
Digital Records Accepted
Most state S&E Acts now permit digital/electronic maintenance of registers, provided records are tamper-proof, backed up, and producible in printed form on demand by inspectors.
Employee Register (Muster Roll)
Name, father's name, address, designation, date of joining, date of birth, nature of employment, UAN, ESIC IP number. Must be updated within 3 days of any change.
S&E Act / S&E Rules
Attendance Register
Daily attendance records including time-in, time-out, and overtime hours. HRMS/biometric data is acceptable if exportable. Retain for minimum 5 years.
Factories Act / S&E Act
Wage Register
Monthly wages paid to each employee, all deductions made, net wages paid, mode of payment, signature/acknowledgement. Must be maintained in prescribed format under applicable state rules.
Payment of Wages Act
Leave Records
Earned Leave (EL), Casual Leave (CL), Sick Leave (SL), Maternity Leave balances and transactions for each employee. Leave encashment records. Annual leave carry-forward calculations.
S&E Act / Factories Act
Overtime Register
Record employee-wise overtime hours and overtime wages paid (typically 2x normal rate). Ensure employees are not working beyond prescribed daily/weekly limits without compensation.
Factories Act / S&E Act
EPF / ESIC Contribution Records
Maintain ECR (Electronic Challan cum Return) copies, challan receipts, and EPFO/ESIC portal transaction records. Retain for minimum 7 years.
EPF Act / ESI Act
Separation / Exit Records
Maintain record of all separations: resignation letters, acceptance, notice periods served/waived, full & final settlement calculations, relieving letters, experience certificates issued.
S&E Act
Accident Register
Record all workplace accidents, near-misses, and occupational diseases. Include date, nature of injury, treatment provided, lost work days. Notify labour authorities for fatal/serious accidents.
Factories Act / State OSH Rules
SECTION 07
Health, Safety & Welfare
Workplace safety, maternity benefits, and employee well-being obligations
High Priority
Section Progress
0%
Workplace Safety Policy
Written safety policy covering emergency procedures, fire evacuation plan, first aid protocols, and hazard communication. Must be communicated to all employees. Review annually.
OSH Rules
First Aid & Medical Facilities
Maintain adequately stocked first aid box (per Rules: 1 box per 150 employees). Designate trained first aider. Display emergency contact numbers including ambulance and nearest hospital.
Factories Act §45
Maternity Benefits Compliance
26 weeks paid maternity leave for first two children (12 weeks for third+). 8 weeks pre-natal. Creche facility if 50+ employees. No termination during maternity leave. Medical bonus ₹3,500 if no hospitalization benefits.
Maternity Benefit Act 2017₹2,000–₹10,000 + imprisonment
Work Hours Compliance
IT/ITES employees (S&E Act): typically 9 hrs/day, 48 hrs/week. Maximum 12 hrs with overtime compensation. Mandatory weekly off. Night shift rules applicable for women employees under state regulations.
State S&E Acts
Fire Safety & Emergency Preparedness
Valid fire NOC from Fire Department. Fire extinguishers installed and inspected quarterly. Fire evacuation drills at least twice a year. Emergency exit signs and lighting functional.
State Fire Services Acts
Mental Health & Employee Assistance
New expectation under OSH Code (pending): consider employee mental health. Growing judicial emphasis on employer duty of care. Consider EAP (Employee Assistance Programme) and mental health days.
EMERGING 2026
Group Mediclaim / Health Insurance
While not legally mandatory for all employers, increasingly expected. If ESIC applies, it substitutes. For ESIC-exempt employees (salary >₹21,000), best practice is to provide group health insurance.
Creche Facility (if 50+ female employees)
Under Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act 2017: creche within 500m radius or within the premises. Mother entitled to 4 visits per day including break. Maintain creche register.
Maternity Benefit Act 2017 §11A

DPDP Act 2023 — Rules Expected 2025–26
The Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 is expected to be operationalised via Rules in 2025-26. Startups must prepare data processing frameworks, consent mechanisms, and data principal rights infrastructure now.
DPDP Act — Data Fiduciary Framework
Map all personal data collected from employees. Identify purpose of processing for each data element. Implement consent framework. Appoint Data Protection Officer if required. Establish data breach response protocol (72-hour notification requirement).
DPDP Act 2023NEW 2026Penalty: up to ₹250 Cr
Employee Privacy Notice & Data Rights
Issue comprehensive privacy notice to employees explaining what data is collected, how it's used, retention period, and their rights (access, correction, erasure). Must be in simple, understandable language.
DPDP Act 2023 §5NEW 2026
Remote Work / WFH Policy
Document eligibility, work hours expectations, equipment provisioning, data security requirements, expense reimbursements, and performance monitoring for remote/hybrid employees. Address S&E Act applicability for home-based workers.
BEST PRACTICE
Gig Worker Classification & Compliance
Correctly classify gig workers, freelancers, and independent contractors vs. employees. Misclassification risk: retrospective EPF, ESIC, TDS, and labour law obligations. Ensure proper contracts for all gig engagements. Monitor Code on Social Security (gig worker provisions).
Code on Social Security 2020GIGS 2026Retrospective liability
Background Verification (BGV) Process
Conduct BGV for all employees: address verification, criminal record check (where permissible), education/employment history verification. Obtain written consent before BGV. DPDP-compliant data handling of BGV data.
DPDP Act 2023
AI Tools in HR — Bias & Transparency Compliance
If using AI for hiring, performance evaluation, or promotions: document decision criteria, ensure non-discrimination, maintain audit trails. Emerging regulatory focus globally; Indian judicial guidance expected.
EMERGING 2026
IT & BYOD Security Policy
Define acceptable use of company IT systems, BYOD guidelines, data classification, encryption requirements, and incident reporting. Employees to sign acknowledgment. Critical for data protection compliance.
IT Act 2000 / DPDP 2023
Social Media & Communication Policy
Define guidelines for employee conduct on social media — confidentiality obligations, brand representation, prohibited content, and consequences of violations. Include whistleblower protections.

Audit & HR Governance
Systems and processes to maintain sustained compliance and governance excellence
Governance
Section Progress

Quarterly HR Compliance Audit
Internal audit of payroll accuracy, statutory filings, document completeness, and policy adherence. Create audit checklist aligned with this document. Address findings within 30 days. Report to founders/board.
Quarterly
Annual External HR Audit
Engage an independent labour law consultant or HR compliance firm for annual audit. Particularly critical before funding rounds, IPO, or M&A due diligence. Address all findings proactively.
Annual
Compliance Calendar & Tracker
Maintain a live compliance calendar with all statutory deadlines, responsible owners, and status tracking. Set automated reminders 30 and 7 days before each deadline. Share with finance and legal teams.
Vendor / Contractor Compliance Checks
As principal employer, verify that all third-party contractors (facility management, security, IT support) comply with EPF, ESIC, Minimum Wages, and POSH obligations for their workers. Collect compliance certificates quarterly.
CLRA Act 1970Quarterly
Digital Documentation & Secure Backup
All HR documents (contracts, returns, challans, registers) stored in encrypted digital form. 3-2-1 backup rule: 3 copies, 2 media types, 1 off-site/cloud. Access controlled with audit logs. Retention policy per statutory requirements (5–8 years).
Grievance Redressal Mechanism
Establish formal grievance mechanism with designated HR contact, defined timelines (acknowledge in 3 days, resolve in 30 days), and escalation path. Document all grievances and resolutions. Separate from POSH complaint channel.
Industrial Employment Act
Board / Management HR Compliance Reporting
Present HR compliance status to board/management quarterly. Include: pending filings, resolved grievances, POSH updates, audit findings, and upcoming regulatory changes. Escalate critical risks immediately.
HR Policy Annual Review & Update
Review all HR policies annually for legal changes, best practices, and business evolution. Minimum review triggers: new labour code notifications, court judgments, SEBI/MCA circulars, and annual before April 1.
